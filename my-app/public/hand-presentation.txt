Executive Summary
HAND (Human Action for Need and Development) is pioneering a comprehensive smart village
revolution across Telangana and Andhra Pradesh, demonstrating exceptional impact through
integrated development approaches. Our work encompasses five critical pillars: skill
development, smart village transformation, education, healthcare, and clean energy/water
solutions.
Since 2020, HAND has reached 85 villages, directly benefiting 18,000 people while training
6,000 students and empowering 4,500 women. Our unique approach combines traditional
community development with modern technology, creating sustainable pathways out of poverty
while preserving cultural heritage.
Key Achievements:
135 healthcare camps conducted with focus on oral cancer awareness
1,570+ films produced by students showcasing creative potential
Water purification plants installed benefiting thousands
Women's employment through organic plate manufacturing
Moringa product development addressing malnutrition

The Smart Village Challenge
Rural India faces a complex web of interconnected challenges that require innovative,
technology-driven solutions. In Telangana and Andhra Pradesh, where HAND operates, these
challenges are particularly acute:

Current Rural Realities
Only 67% of rural populations have adequate healthcare access [1]
50% shortage of medical staff in rural health centers [2]
Digital literacy remains at 25% in rural areas [3]

Women's participation in rural employment is only 27% nationally [4]
Access to safe drinking water varies between 66-68% across our target states [5]

The Urgency of Action
With 908.8 million people residing in rural India [6] and 65% of the country's population in
villages [7] , the transformation of rural communities represents both India's greatest challenge
and its most significant opportunity. Smart villages can serve as growth centers that reduce
migration pressure on cities while creating sustainable livelihoods locally [7] .
